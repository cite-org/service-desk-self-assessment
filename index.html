Processes & Best Practices is one of your weaker areas. Consider the following questions:
  1. Do you have a formal ticketing system in place? If not, an investment in this kind of software would dramatically improve your department's productivity.
  2. If you do have ticketing software, is it missing key features that would make it more helpful? What features would be most needed?
  3. Does your department need to invest more time into documenting Help Desk processes? This would help keep all staff on the same page and improve training new hires.
  4. What framework could you use that would provide a more formal structure for the Help Desk department? ITIL is just one of many. Even an internally developed framework could be helpful.
Organizational Integration is one of your weaker areas. Consider the following questions:
  1. Outside of your department, who might be an advocate to help you become more integrated with the rest of the campus? Set up a meeting with them to talk about improvements here.
  2. What structure could you create to help give direction to regular meetings that connect Help Desk to the needs of campus? Consider designing a meeting template to ensure all areas are covered.
  3. How could you better train new teachers and staff during the onboarding process, helping them understand the role and processes of Help Desk?
Technology Optimization is one of your weaker areas. Consider the following questions:
  1. What are some tasks/requests you find yourself addressing over and over again? How might these be automated to minimize your service time?
  2. What do you imagine a helpful and robust knowledge base could look like? Could your department invest time and resources towards building a video library of resolutions to common problems?
  3. What regular reports would be most helpful in assessing the productivity and efficiency of the Help Desk? Does your current software allow this?
Staff Development is one of your weaker areas. Consider the following questions:
  1. What would be some areas of expertise your department could use right now? Might there be training programs for that skill you can encourage staff to participate in?
  2. What feedback would be most helpful to recieve from Help Desk staff? What feedback would you like to give to Help Desk staff?
  3. Do you have a formal evaluation questionnaire? Consider a template that measures many aspects of staff knowledge and skills. Set a goal for regular evaluations.
Information Management is one of your weaker areas. Consider the following questions:
  1. How is institutional knowledge of your help desk stored in your school? How could information be more easily accessible to help resolve indicents and problems?
  2. What kind of meeting or staff development opportunity could allow your Help Desk personnel come together and learn from each other? Could this be scheduled regularly?
  3. Might it be worth formalizing an SLA between the Help Desk and the school overall? What types of service would be essential to communicate to others outside the department?
